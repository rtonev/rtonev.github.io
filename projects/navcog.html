Summary

During my first 6 weeks as a IBMer, I participated in a design bootcamp, aimed at introducing me to Enterprise Design Thinking and the IBM culture and values. During that time, I was part of a team of 9 designers and product managers, with whom I worked on an accessibility project for IBM Research. The project is called NavCog, which is a mobile solution that aims to make airports more accessible for travellers who are blind. As part of our work, we redesigned NavCog to be a voice-first solution and added important features that would make independent travelling more possible for people who are blind.

Problem Statement

Our group was tasked with making the existing NavCog prototype more useful for blind people travelling via airplanes. The solution was aimed to be potentially deployed at multiple airports and potentially exapanded in the future to other venues, including the Tokyo 2020 Olympics. The redesign was aimed at improving the value proposition and usability of the tool. We were to meet members of the project's target audience and work with them to gain insight on how NavCog could be improved.
